# Medicus Suite — the road to 10/10 and a £200m product — 2026-06-21

> Two panels asked the same question on the same renders (v3.126.6): **The
> Practice** (synthetic clinical staff — usability/trust) and **Vogue** (a
> world-class design house — craft/premium feel). Both are synthetic heuristics,
> not real users. This note merges their answers into one ladder.

## Where it stands today

- **The Practice: mean ≈ 7.1/10** (Margaret 7, Tom 7, Janet 7, Eileen 7, Geoff
  7.5) — up from **5.7** at the start of this session. Usable, trusted on the
  daily path, but the reconciling roles still find numbers they cannot defend.
- **Vogue: ~6.5/10 craft** against Linear / Stripe / Raycast. "A genuinely good,

So: a strong, trusted instrument that is **one focused number-integrity pass away
from a usability 10**, and a **multi-week design program away from a craft 10**.
The two ladders are different work.

## Ladder 1 — to a usability 10 (The Practice)

The convergent theme is **number integrity**: a reconciling role abandons the
tool the moment one figure contradicts another.

1. **Single-source every count; never show the same datum two ways.** *(Tom,
   Janet — blocker)* Condor's "WR: 0" must never sit beside "6 waiting"; the two
   unrelated "23"s (demand vs the 0–100 index) must each carry a unit on the
   number itself ("23 requests" vs "Pressure 23/100"). **L.**
2. **Make every compound figure self-explaining.** *(Janet)* "Cap: 30/0" needs
   plain words ("30 slots / 0 spare"); "at capacity" must reconcile on-screen
   with "30 free slots" (they measure different things — say so); show the
   AMBER-band arithmetic (37 vs 30 → index → floor). **M.**
3. **Promote the buried alert; lead each card with the action.** *(Margaret,
   Tom)* "Oldest unanswered 4h" should be a visible count, not grey footnote;
   plainer labels (Morning/Afternoon, explain "On the day"). **M.**
4. **Provenance on the quiet states.** *(Eileen)* A dated "last checked HH:MM —
   clear" line so a calm Monitoring screen means *checked*, not *not-yet-looked*.
   **M.**
5. **The never-break floor.** *(Eileen — the regression to 4)* an overdue check
   on an opened record must never render as the calm idle state. Guard it with a
   test; this is the one that must never slip. **(already guarded; keep it.)**

Hit 1–4 and the reconciling roles (Tom, Janet) and the floor (Margaret, Eileen)
all move to 9–10. None needs new doctrine.

## Ladder 2 — to a craft 10 and the £200m feel (Vogue)

Ranked by impact on "is this a flagship":

1. **The all-caps monospace label system — Vogue's "one thing". XL + STRATEGIC.**
   Nearly every label is uppercase letter-spaced mono (WAITING ROOM, TRIAGE
   LOAD, BY CLINICIAN). Vogue: "it reads as terminal/avionics *cosplay*, not an
   authored type system... mono is doing both chrome and content, so nothing is
   special." The fix is a real sentence-case sans type ramp with **mono reserved
   strictly for data** (times, counts, indices). **This directly contradicts the
   house DOCTRINE principle #4 ("the dual-voice typography is the brand").** It is
   the single biggest lever to the £200m feel *and* a bet against the current
   identity — **a decision for Dave, not a freehand change.** (See "The pivotal
   call" below.)
2. **Actually converge the pills. L.** Still 3–4 pill grammars on one screen
   (triage-strip pills vs TRIAGE-LOAD card chips vs WR chips vs count chips). One
   pill, documented variants. (This session's G4 only touched the ampm/condor
   outliers — the lineage split remains.)
3. **An owned data-viz language. L.** The Condor half-doughnut "looks like a
   Chart.js default — the one component that looks bought, not made." Replace with
   an owned capacity meter (a fine radial tick scale, or a horizontal bar with a
   threshold marker) whose geometry echoes the reticle mark. Also resolves the
   gauge-number confusion in Ladder 1.
4. **A signature motion beat. M.** Nothing implies state transition. One
   restrained language — numbers settling on load, the demand band *easing*
   neutral→amber over ~400ms, a soft pulse on "Live" — reduced-motion guarded.
   "Would do more for perceived quality than any static fix."
5. **Deploy the brand beyond the logo. M.** The reticle is inert in one 16px
   square; propagate its geometry into the gauge ticks, focus rings, the
   empty-state glyph, a loading state — so the product feels *owned* by it.
6. **Lighten the chrome-to-content ratio. M.** Seven fully-bordered cards =
   heavy box-drawing; hairline dividers / borderless sections read more
   expensive for the same data.
7. **Empty-state craft & dark-mode finesse. M each.** The salmon WR strip muddies
   to maroon in dark; re-tune each accent for the dark substrate. Empty states
   are honest but plain — the place to show personality.
8. **Grid rhythm on one baseline. S.** Inter-card spacing and hero-vs-card
   padding aren't on a single 4/8px grid; tightening is "what makes Stripe feel
   machined."

## The pivotal call (for Dave)

Vogue's headline — **retire the all-caps mono voice for a sentence-case sans
ramp** — is the biggest single move to "£200m authored", and it is a **direct
challenge to the documented brand doctrine**, which holds the dual mono/sans
voice as *the* identity. This is not a change to make freehand. The options:

- **(A) Hold the doctrine** — the mono voice is a deliberate, owned "clinical
  instrument" choice; double down on craft *within* it (pills, gauge, motion,
  brand propagation) and accept Vogue's critique as a matter of taste.
- **(B) Take Vogue's bet** — commission a type-ramp redesign, demote mono to
  data only. Highest upside to "premium/authored", but it re-skins every surface
  and rewrites principle #4.
- **(C) Hybrid** — keep mono for the machine voice (nav, metadata, data) but move
  *section headers and secondary labels* to sentence-case sans, so the caps stop
  carrying everything. Lower risk, captures much of the gain.

My recommendation: **(C)** — it answers Vogue's substantive point (mono
overused) without discarding the identity, and it is reversible. But it is your
identity, so it is your call.

## When is it 10/10 and a £200m product?

- **Usability 10** — when a practice manager cannot find a single contradicting
  or unexplained number, the technophobe is told in plain words what needs them
  now, and the safety floor is provably unbreakable. That is **Ladder 1: a
  focused number-integrity + plain-language pass (~1–2 weeks of design-crit
  work).**
- **Craft 10 / £200m** — when the suite reads as *authored by one hand*: a
  resolved type system (the pivotal call), one pill, an owned data-viz language,
  a single motion signature, and a brand that propagates beyond the logo. That is
  **Ladder 2: a multi-week design program**, gated on the type-system decision.

Today it is **~7 usability / ~6.5 craft**. Ladder 1 is in-house and ready to run.
Ladder 2 is mostly in-house too, but its biggest rung needs your ruling first.
