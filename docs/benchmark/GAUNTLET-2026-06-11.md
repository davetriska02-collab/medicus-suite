# The Gauntlet: Medicus Suite vs the UK GP add-on market

**Run date:** 2026-06-11 · **Subject:** Medicus Suite v3.55.0 · **Framing:** internal tool, stay sharp (ideas worth stealing, not procurement)
**Scope (Phase 1):** scored for a whole-practice buyer; comparables are GP clinical-system add-ons; dimensions ranked (1) clinical safety depth, (2) workflow/UX fit, (3) feature depth and utility.
**Method:** five research agents over primary sources (vendor docs/KBs, G-Cloud listings, NHS/ICB documents, Companies House, published trials). Every vendor site (Ardens, PCIT, Eclipse, FDB, Edenbridge, GP Automate) blocked direct fetches (HTTP 403); claims are sourced from indexed page extracts and framework documents and labelled accordingly. Medicus Suite scored from its own code at v3.55.0 by the same standard: roadmap = absent.

---

## Executive summary

Medicus Suite is the only product found that combines per-patient clinical safety alerting, free-text triage red-flagging, reception decision support, demand/capacity ops and an offline record visualiser in one tool; every commercial competitor owns exactly one of those columns. On the dimension Dave ranked first (clinical safety depth) the suite's rule engine is competitive with the market leaders and uniquely carries STOPP/START and ACB scoring, which neither Ardens nor FDB surface. The market position is protected less by feature depth than by host: the suite is the only deep, real-time, in-record safety layer running on Medicus Health, where incumbents are absent or dashboard-only.

**Three most dangerous competitors:** Ardens (breadth, 87% practice claim, and the only incumbent already integrated with Medicus via IM1); FDB OptimiseRx (the in-consultation PINCER CDS standard, with FDB Multilex already inside the Medicus host itself since July 2025); Accurx (owns the triage front door in 98% of practices, with 2026 roadmap items creeping towards request classification).

**Single biggest gap:** the recall loop. The suite alerts brilliantly but does not manage the cycle that follows: Ardens (Diary Recall), PCIT (OneRecall/OneMonitoring) and Eclipse (Radar worklists + write-back) all close alert → invite → book → re-test; we stop at the chip and an Action Pack.

**Single biggest opportunity:** the free-text triage red-flag engine. Nothing in the market reads inbound request text for MH crisis, sepsis, DKA or cauda equina phrasing. Accurx's screening is a patient-facing checkbox; PCIT's pre-triage is routing. This is genuine white space and it is already shipped: deepen it.

## Gap analysis

### Table stakes we are missing
1. **Recall loop management** (alert → invite → book → close): Ardens, PCIT and Eclipse all ship it; we stop at the chip.
2. **Coded-refset precision**: every serious competitor runs on SNOMED refsets/business rules; our substring approximation is the documented weak joint (mitigated, not closed, by Keeper + tests).
3. **Compliance artefact stack** (DTAC, DSPT, certified DCB0129): irrelevant while internal, fatal the day the suite is shared beyond named users. Accurx publishes its hazard log; ours is 19+ versions stale (audit T4, still open).
4. **Resilient data feed**: IM1-style structured extraction vs our DOM scraping. Not available to an extension; the extraction-health canary work (audit T10) is the realistic mitigation.

### Parity
- Drug-monitoring rule depth per drug ≈ Ardens/FDB coverage. QOF indicator tracking ≈ Ardens/PCIT (they add contract-finance framing we don't need). Demand/capacity dashboards ≈ APEX-lite (their 3NA metric and clinician appraisal report are worth stealing). CQC-style evidence views ≈ partial both sides.

### White space (verified across all seven)
1. **Free-text clinical triage of inbound request text** — unique to us.
2. **Reception red-flag decision support** with structured escalation (999/duty tiers) — unique (Accurx = checkbox, PCIT = routing).
3. **STOPP/START + ACB at point of care** — unique.
4. **Real-time in-record safety HUD on Medicus** — unique today; Ardens is the only incumbent with a foothold (dashboards confirmed, in-record depth unverified).
5. **Offline PDF record visualiser** (continuity, eFI, timeline) — unique, no commercial pull for incumbents.

Where a competitor is simply better on a ranked dimension: **OptimiseRx beats us on interaction-alert delivery** (fires inside the prescribing act itself; ours render alongside the record) and on **evidence base** (RCT lineage vs our internal tests). **Ardens beats us on breadth-per-pound and compliance posture.** **Accurx beats us on the triage front door** (patients submit through it; we only read what arrives).
