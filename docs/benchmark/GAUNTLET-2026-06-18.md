# The Gauntlet: Medicus Suite clinical core vs the UK GP CDS / QOF market

**Run date:** 2026-06-18 · **Subject:** Medicus Suite v3.117.x (flagship clinical core: Sentinel drug-monitoring, QOF, free-text triage, result-triage, reception CDS) · **Buyer lens:** practice / PCN procurement · **Top dimensions (Phase 1):** (1) in-consultation UX and speed, (2) clinical-safety depth and correctness.
**Comparables in scope:** native EMR features (EMIS Web, TPP SystmOne, Medicus) and third-party GP CDS / QOF tools (Ardens, FDB OptimiseRx, ScriptSwitch, PINCER/CHART, Abtrace, C the Signs). Anima carried as an adjacent total-triage reference.
**Method:** seven primary-source research agents, 2026-06-18. **Evidence caveat that colours the whole report:** every commercial and most NHS domains (ardens.org.uk, fdbhealth.co.uk, tpp-uk.com, emishealth.com, medicus.health, abtrace, cthesigns, digital.nhs.uk) returned HTTP 403 to direct fetch. All competitor feature claims therefore rest on search-engine extracts of those primary pages plus reachable NHS/parliamentary/peer-reviewed documents, not full-page reads. Confidence is labelled per claim. Medicus Suite is scored from its own code at v3.117.x by the same standard: roadmap items are absent, not shipped.
**Relationship to the prior run:** this complements `GAUNTLET-2026-06-11.md` (whole add-on market, v3.55.0). It narrows to the clinical core, switches the buyer lens to procurement, and adds four comparators the prior run did not column: PINCER/CHART, Abtrace, C the Signs, and native EMR depth (EMIS/SystmOne/Medicus). Diff notes against the prior run are flagged inline.

## Executive summary

Medicus Suite's clinical core is the broadest in-record intelligence layer in this comparison, and the only product that combines per-patient drug-monitoring, QOF tracking, free-text request triage, result-triage and reception red-flag support in one tool on the live record. Every commercial competitor owns one band: OptimiseRx and ScriptSwitch own prescribing, PINCER owns retrospective prescribing-safety audit, C the Signs owns cancer pathways, Abtrace owns monitoring-plus-QOF case-finding, Ardens owns breadth-of-templates. On the two dimensions chosen in Phase 1 the picture splits. On in-consultation UX the suite is at parity, not ahead: Ardens, OptimiseRx and C the Signs are all embedded in the screen too, and the native EMR is the screen. On clinical-safety depth the suite's rule breadth is competitive and its STOPP/START and ACB scoring are unique, but it trails on the part of "depth" a procurement panel actually scores: published evidence and coded precision.

The uncomfortable truth under a procurement lens is that the suite is the only entrant here that is not a procurement-ready product. It carries no DTAC, no DSPT, no certified DCB0129 stack, it is Medicus-only against competitors who cover EMIS and SystmOne and Vision, it matches on substring approximation where incumbents run on SNOMED refsets, and it has no published evidence where PINCER has a Lancet RCT. It would lose a formal procurement today on assurance, reach and evidence despite winning on breadth and being the only deep in-record layer that runs on Medicus at all.

**Three most dangerous competitors:** (1) **Ardens** , breadth, native in-screen alerts on EMIS and SystmOne, a full compliance stack (DTAC, DCB0129/0160, DSPT, ISO 27001, Cyber Essentials Plus), and the only incumbent with a Medicus foothold (high-risk-drug searches listed as available on Medicus via IM1). (2) **FDB OptimiseRx** , the in-consultation prescribing-safety standard with PINCER lineage and a published 500m-pound savings narrative, whose FDB Multilex engine already sits inside Medicus natively. (3) **Abtrace** , the closest functional twin (monitoring plus QOF case-finding plus recall) and, unlike us, a UKCA-marked medical device with the assurance posture we lack.

**Single biggest gap:** procurement readiness, the compliance / evidence / EMR-reach triad. This is a deliberate diff from the prior run, which named the recall loop; under a procurement buyer the assurance gap outranks it.

**Single biggest opportunity:** the Medicus-native, whole-loop, in-record position. Medicus exposes no third-party UI-injection surface (verified by inference across three native-EMR research streams) and the live estate is roughly four practices, so no incumbent can or will inject a deep in-screen layer onto Medicus. That, plus the free-text request-triage engine that no competitor's data model even sees, is genuine white space.

## Gap analysis

### Table stakes we are missing (these lose a procurement comparison regardless of cleverness elsewhere)

1. **Compliance artefact stack (DTAC, DSPT, certified DCB0129).** Ardens has the full set; Abtrace and C the Signs are registered medical devices; the native EMRs carry DCB0129 as systems of record. The suite has a hazard log, DPIA, CSO declaration and a DTAC-status note already, which is further along than the market assumes, but it holds no DTAC pass, no DSPT, no certified clinical-safety case, and is explicitly not a medical device. Under a procurement buyer this is the single biggest gap and a hard gate.
2. **Recall loop (alert to invite to book to re-test).** Ardens (Diary Recall), Abtrace (automated recall) and the native EMRs all close it; C the Signs closes it for FIT. We stop at the chip plus an Action Pack.
3. **Coded-refset precision.** Every serious competitor runs on SNOMED refsets and QOF business rules. Our substring approximation is the documented weak joint, mitigated by the Keeper rule-currency process and brand-coverage tests, not closed.
4. **EMR reach.** Medicus-only against competitors who cover EMIS, SystmOne and often Vision. For a PCN or ICB buying across a mixed estate this is close to disqualifying outside Medicus practices. It is a by-design consequence of the augmentation model, not a defect, but a procurement panel scores it as a gap.
5. **Published evidence.** PINCER has a Lancet RCT and a national time-series; C the Signs has peer-reviewed breadth and detection-rate data; OptimiseRx has health-economic savings. We have internal tests only.

### Parity (level, one line each)

- Drug-monitoring rule depth per drug is comparable to Ardens and Abtrace.
- QOF indicator tracking is comparable to Ardens, which adds contract-finance framing we do not need.
- In-record live surfacing is level with Ardens, OptimiseRx and C the Signs; the native EMR is the screen itself.
- Result handling is level-to-ahead: native EMRs file and mark, we add explicit severity and under-prioritised chips, but C the Signs beats everyone inside the cancer/FIT lane.

### White space (nobody covers it well)

1. **Free-text clinical triage of inbound request text.** Unique. C the Signs reads the structured record for cancer risk; Anima and AccuRx are structured questionnaires; the CDS vendors never see inbound free text at all. No competitor's data model even ingests it.
2. **STOPP/START plus ACB at the point of care.** Unique across all eight.
3. **Multi-domain single tool on the live record.** Unique; every competitor is a single band and monetises per SKU.
4. **A real-time in-record layer on Medicus.** Unique and structurally protected (see stress test).
5. **General result-triage queue chips beyond cancer/FIT.** Near white space: C the Signs does FIT only, native EMRs only file, nobody else does general abnormal / under-prioritised result triage.

### Where a competitor is simply better on a ranked dimension

- **Clinical-safety depth, evidence component:** PINCER and OptimiseRx beat us decisively. PINCER has independent RCT evidence; we have none.
- **Clinical-safety depth, delivery of prescribing alerts:** OptimiseRx beats us, it fires inside the prescribing act; ours render alongside the record.
- **In-consultation UX, raw embedding:** parity at best. OptimiseRx, C the Signs and native alerts are as embedded or more so. Our edge is breadth in one pane, not depth of embedding.
- **Procurement posture:** Ardens, Abtrace and C the Signs are all materially ahead on certification, EMR reach and evidence.

**Stress-test verdict:** L2 is the only leapfrog with a hard structural barrier (no UI-injection surface on Medicus plus a sub-scale estate) and is the bet to press. L1 and L3 are genuine today but copyable inside 12-24 months by the reach players, so they are speed plays, not moats. The Match tier, especially M1, is what actually changes the procurement verdict; without it the breadth advantage never gets scored because the suite does not clear the assurance gate.
